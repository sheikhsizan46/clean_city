Your Guide to Smart Recycling

Tip 1: Know Your Plastics

Take a moment to check the number printed inside the recycling symbol on plastic items. Most councils accept number 1 and number 2 plastics as they are easier to process. This quick habit helps ensure your recycling is handled correctly.

Tip 2: Clean, Dry and Loose

A small amount of food left inside a container can spoil a whole recycling batch. Give jars and bottles a quick rinse, let them dry and place them loosely in the bin. It helps recycling centres sort materials more easily.

Tip 3: The Scrunch Test

When you are unsure about a shiny wrapper, try the scrunch test. If it stays scrunched, it is likely aluminium foil and can be recycled when clean. If it pops open again, it belongs in general waste because it is usually plastic film.

Tip 4: What About Pizza Boxes

Pizza boxes often cause confusion. If the cardboard has grease or cheese on it, it cannot be recycled. Tear off the clean top half to recycle and put the greasy part in general waste or compost if your area allows.
